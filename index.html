I’ve been deep in the AI rabbit hole for a while now and one of my favorite tests for new AI models has been checking how well they can generate original “Bermanisms.” If you don’t know, a Bermanism is a nickname given to an athlete (or anyone really) where some cultural reference is inserted into the name. It’s a form of clever wordplay that, when done well, can be satisfying.

Here are some classic examples from Chris Berman (long-time ESPN broadcaster) himself:
Bert “Be Home” Blyleven
Ben “Winter” Coates
Mike “You’re in Good Hands with” Alstott
Roberto "Remember the " Alomar

After years of bad performance on this benchmark, the AIs have been improving in the last few months. Open AI models still seem to struggle. Anthropic’s best model is better, but the best model for Bermanisms currently is Google’s Gemini 3.1 Pro. I’m not a big fan of the Google models, but for this one task it does the best for some reason.
